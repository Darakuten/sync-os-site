01 · Body-centric UI

The body is the primary axis, not the device.

Existing biosignal tools force you to think in channels and probes. Sync OS inverts it: a single anatomical canvas with each modality docked to where it physically lives — EEG at the scalp, ECG at the chest, PPG at the wrist, EDA at the palm. Streams flow out from the body, not into a spreadsheet.

02 · Real-time fusion

Eight modalities reduced to five states.

The Sync.OS core continuously fuses raw signals into five interpretable scores — stress · emotion · fatigue · attention · cogload. The dashboard shows these states updating live, so any product or clinician on top of Sync OS reasons over the same shared vocabulary.

03 · Closed-loop intervention

Sensing only matters if you act on it.

Intervention is a first-class output. Trigger Relax / Focus presets, drive haptic watches, audio entrainment, light, and agent calls — then watch the states shift on the same screen. Sync OS is the substrate that makes the loop short enough to feel.

Five states

The vocabulary of state.

Sync OS reduces dozens of raw biosignals into the same five interpretable scores surfaced in the live console — the smallest vocabulary that lets software meaningfully respond to a human in the loop.

01 · Stress
STRESS
Acute autonomic load. Derived from LF/HF ratio, electrodermal response and heart rate.
0.34
02 · Emotion
EMOTION
Affective valence. Inferred from frontal alpha asymmetry and blink dynamics.
0.62
03 · Fatigue
FATIGUE
Mental fatigue. Combines alpha/beta ratio, respiration rate and tonic heart rate.
0.28
04 · Attention
ATTENTION
Focused engagement. Driven by beta power, alpha/beta ratio and blink suppression.
0.81
05 · CogLoad
COGLOAD
Cognitive load (n-back equivalent). Beta activity, heart rate and LF/HF together.
0.46
